Telegram-native CRM

Your CRM is failing you. Your phone already has the answer.

2026-06-27 · 6 min read

You spent $40k on Salesforce last year. Your reps log into it twice a week. By the end of the quarter, the contacts that actually moved a deal lived in someone's Telegram inbox — half labelled, half remembered, all out of reach when someone on the team asked "who was that person from the ZK panel?"

This isn't a workflow problem. It's a fundamental mismatch between how the CRM was designed and how high-volume networking actually happens.

The CRM decay problem

Nucleus Research has tracked CRM data decay for over a decade. Their finding, replicated by every serious CRM analyst since: contact data rots at roughly 25–30% per year. Phone numbers change, titles shift, people move companies. By year three, a "clean" 10,000-row database is functionally 4,000 rows — and the ones that matter most (the ones you met last week) were never entered in the first place.

The Salesforce 2026 State of Sales report flags the same problem from a different angle: 51% of sales leaders say "tech silos" are blocking their AI strategy. Not lack of AI. Silos. The system of record for contacts isn't where anyone is actually working.

Salesforce's own internal research has, for years, pegged the average rep at spending only about 24% of their week actually selling. The rest is admin, logging, searching, typing names from memory three days after the event.

The CRM assumes the contact lives somewhere else first. Every second you spend typing it in is a second your brain is already moving on.

Where the contacts actually live

Telegram crossed 1 billion monthly active users in 2025. In crypto and the founder world, the number is functionally "everyone you meet at an event." The contact doesn't have to be captured into anything. It already lives in the chat you're in, on the device you're holding, the moment the handshake ends.

The capture is three seconds: open their profile, tap forward, send it to a bot. No app switch. No form. No remembering the company name on the flight home.

The data point that flips the script: the same Salesforce 2026 report says 94% of sales leaders now consider AI agents essential to growth. But AI is only as good as the input. If the input is a sales rep typing names from memory three days after the event, the AI gets garbage.

The contact has to be captured at the moment of meeting, in a tool that already lives where you already are.

The chat-native system

That forward-to-bot pattern is what trce is built around. It does three things a CRM can't, because a CRM was never designed for the moment of meeting:

  • Capture at the source — within three seconds of the handshake, the contact is indexed with the conversation context, the booth, the afterparty
  • Zero data entry — no typing, no tag selection, no "what's their company again"
  • Search where you already are — type a keyword in Telegram and get the person, the note, and the place you met them

You don't need to cancel Salesforce. You need to stop pretending it's where your fresh contacts live. The CRM is the system you log into. The chat is the system you live in. When the contact capture happens in chat, the database stays alive without anyone touching a CRM.

The contact card never died. It just moved into the chat window.

Try it

Then a week later, search your bot for "restaking" or "L2" or whatever you talked about. If the person appears with the booth and the conversation note attached — that's the system working.

The CRM will still be there for pipeline, deals, and renewals. But the contact capture layer belongs somewhere it actually fits — in the chat.
