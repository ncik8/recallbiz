How to survive a 500-person crypto event without losing a single contact

Nick Williams · 2026-06-15 · 8 min read

You walked into Token2049 at 9am. By 6pm you've met 60 people, taken 12 paper business cards, scanned 30 Telegram QRs, and your pockets are full. By the time you get home, you remember maybe 5 of those names. By next week, you remember 1.

This is the death of most crypto networking. The introduction happens, the contact gets saved in some app you never open again, and the relationship dies before it starts.

The system we built

For Token2049 this year, we ran an experiment. Every time someone shared a Telegram QR with us, we forwarded it to @trceiobot within 5 seconds. By the end of the event, we had:

  • 247 contacts saved, each tagged with the booth/afterparty where we met them
  • 47 contacts from one specific talk (the L2 sequencer panel)
  • Searchable database by company, by role, by what we talked about
  • Follow-up drafts ready within 24 hours of the event ending

Three weeks later, we still remember everyone. That's the point.

The 30-second introduction

The hardest part isn't the tool — it's the introduction. After 50 introductions at a crypto event, your brain goes into autopilot. You say "Hi, I'm X, I work on Y" and immediately forget the other person's name.

The trick: anchor on one specific thing they said. Not their company. Not their role. Something they claimed, something they asked, something they disagreed with.

Examples:

  • "You're the one who thinks ZK rollups are overhyped, right?"
  • "You asked me about L2 sequencer decentralisation at the panel"
  • "You disagreed with the restaking pitch — why?"

That single anchor is enough to recall the whole conversation when you see their name in your database three weeks later.

The forwarding habit

The second trick is making the "save this contact" step take less than 5 seconds. If it takes 30 seconds, you stop doing it after the 10th person.

That's why trce lives in Telegram. You don't open an app. You don't type a name into a form. You forward an image. Three taps. Done.

The follow-up

Database collected. Now what?

The 24-hour window after an event is critical. If you message people within a day, your reply rate is 40-60%. If you wait a week, it's 10-15%.

But 247 follow-ups is too many to write by hand. Even if each one takes 30 seconds, that's 2 hours of writing.

That's why the next iteration of trce drafts follow-ups for you. You open the bot after the event, tap "draft follow-ups for Token2049", and it produces a 3-sentence message for each contact that references what you talked about. You review, edit the 5% that need personalisation, send the rest.
